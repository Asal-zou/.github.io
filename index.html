About Me

As a Biomedical Engineering graduate, my academic journey has been driven by a profound fascination with the intersection of computer science and data analytics in the realm of medical imaging. My thesis on data analytics and my role as a Research and Development Assistant at a startup have been pivotal experiences that solidified my interest in this field.

Starting my academic journey at Aachen University of Applied Sciences, I went beyond theoretical learning, gaining practical training in diverse areas. Each semester brought specific practical training within each course, like imaging and signal processing, introducing me to Doppler sonography and ultrasound techniques. My studies in general biomedical engineering equipped me with a comprehensive understanding of industry facets and academic possibilities.

In the final one and half years of my studies, I dedicated myself to an in-depth bachelor's thesis, inspired by the captivating experience of exploring research methodologies across different courses. A few months after completing my studies, I had the privilege of publishing and presenting my thesis, titled 'Cost-effectiveness analysis of different anti-VEGF inhibitors as a treatment for neovascular age-related macular degeneration using Markov modeling,' at the DOG Congress.

Motivated by my experience in research and a passion for continuous learning, I joined E-Rain Corp., a startup committed to Innovation and Product Development. This real-world exposure provided insights into the inception of inventions and allowed me to gain hands-on knowledge in conducting market research. In this dynamic environment, I learned the intricacies of transforming innovative ideas into tangible products, deepening my understanding of computer programming and inspiring me to pursue further academic studies.
